Distance Learning Credentials

Credentials are academic degree and certificate awards conferred by a college or university signifying that the student has satisfactorily completed a course of study. The University of Hawaii, through the many Distance Learning programs offered across the ten campuses, provides a pathway for students to confer one of the following awards

Certificate

A certificate is usually two years or less of courses designated by a department. The certificates may include specific courses in a professional/technical field only.

Associate Degree

An associate degree is typically completed in two years of full-time study. However, some distance learning programs may be accelerated, allowing students to finish their degree in less time. These undergraduate programs are offered by the community colleges.

Bachelor’s Degree

A bachelor’s degree takes four years of full-time study to complete. However, some distance learning programs may be accelerated, allowing students to finish their degree in less time.

Post-Baccalaureate Degree

A post baccalaureate certificate ("postbac") is awarded when a student completes courses beyond undergraduate studies in a non-degree granting program. The name literally means "after the bachelor's degree" and allows a student to go deeper or broader in his or her studies.

Graduate Certificate

A graduate certificate, sometimes called a “professional certificate”, is a higher education qualification at the same level as a bachelor's degree but more limited in scope, taking less time to complete - normally between one third and two thirds of an academic year (or full-time equivalent).

Master’s Degree

Master’s degrees are graduate-level programs that typically take two years of additional full-time study after completion of a bachelor’s degree. It shows that a candidate has achieved mastery in a particular field of study.

Doctoral Degree

A doctorate is the highest level of academic degree awarded and can take anywhere from three to eight years (or more) to complete. Some doctoral programs require students to already have a master’s before enrolling, while others can be started directly after completion of undergraduate study.
